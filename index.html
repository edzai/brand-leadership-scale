About the Brand Leadership Scale

What is the Brand Leadership Scale?

The Brand Leadership Scale (BLS) is a validated psychometric tool that measures consumers' perception of a brand's leadership relative to competitors. Developed by Chang and Ko (2014) and published in the Journal of Brand Management, it comprises 12 items across four dimensions rated on a 7-point scale.

What are the four dimensions of brand leadership?

Perceived Quality — consumers' judgment about a product's relative superiority in a market. Perceived Value — consumers' evaluation of relative financial value based on what they give and receive. Perceived Innovativeness — consumers' perception of a brand's capability to embrace new ideas and solutions. Perceived Popularity — consumers' perception of a brand's relative popularity reflected by awareness and consumption.

How is the score interpreted?

Scores range from 1 to 7. A score above 5.5 indicates a Brand Leader; 4.0–5.4 a Brand Contender; 2.5–3.9 a Brand Challenger; below 2.5 a Brand Follower. Dimension scores reveal specific areas of competitive strength or weakness.

Who developed the Brand Leadership Scale?

The BLS was developed by Yonghwan Chang and Yong Jae Ko at the University of Florida and published in 2014: Chang, Y., & Ko, Y.J. (2014). The brand leadership: Scale development and validation. Journal of Brand Management, 21(1), 63–80. doi:10.1057/bm.2013.23.

How do you measure brand leadership?

Brand leadership is measured from the consumer's perspective. Respondents rate a specific brand against competitors across 12 statements on a 7-point Likert scale. Scores are averaged within each of the four dimensions and then combined into an overall brand leadership score, giving a comprehensive picture of the brand's competitive position.
